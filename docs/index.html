General Information

The R computing environment has become one of the most important tools in quantitative research, from computational biology to financial modeling. This short workshop will expose participants to the basic elements of R through an analysis of Divvy bike trip data. The aim is to provide participants with the basic tools to explore the features of R and RStudio on their own, either on the RCC cluster, or on their own computer.

Level: Introductory

Prerequistes: Mac, Linux, or Windows operating system that they have administrative privileges on. An RCC account is helpful, but not required. No previous programming experience is required.

Where: Kathleen A. Zar Room, John Crerar Library, University of Chicago (OpenStreetMap).

Other information: This workshop is an attempt to apply elements of the Software Carpentry approach. See also this article. Please also take a look at the Code of Conduct, and the license information.

Outline

During this workshop, we will explore city-wide biking trends by analyzing the Divvy trip data. We will analyze in more detail the Divvy trip data from a bike station at the University of Chicago.

  1. Setup instructions.

  2. Import and inspect the Divvy station data.

  3. Import and inspect the Divvy trip data.

  4. Create plots from the Divvy station and trip data.

  5. Analyze U of C biking trends from Divvy data.
